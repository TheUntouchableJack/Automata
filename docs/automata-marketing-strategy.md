# Automata — Marketing & Conversion Strategy

## The Mindset

We don't build features and hope people find them. We architect experiences that pull people through a journey — from stranger to evangelist. Every pixel, every word, every interaction is designed to convert.

**We are not building a product. We are building a discovery experience.**

---

## The User Journey

```
AWARENESS → INTEREST → DESIRE → ACTION → ACTIVATION → RETENTION → ADVOCACY
    ↓           ↓          ↓        ↓          ↓           ↓          ↓
 Content    Landing    Social    Free     Onboarding   Value      Referral
  / Ads      Page      Proof    Trial     + Aha!     Delivery    Program
```

Every stage has ONE job: move them to the next stage. Nothing else matters.

## Stage 1: Awareness (They Discover Us)

### Channels
- **Content Marketing:** Medium, LinkedIn, Reddit, X
- **SEO:** "automation for small business", "AI customer relationships", "scale customer communication"
- **Paid:** Targeted ads to SMB owners, solopreneurs, politicians
- **Viral Loops:** User-generated content, case studies, testimonials

### Content Strategy

Every piece of content follows this formula:

1. **Hook** — Pattern interrupt. Say something unexpected.
2. **Problem** — Articulate their pain better than they can.
3. **Agitate** — Make the pain feel urgent.
4. **Solution** — Introduce Automata as the obvious answer.
5. **CTA** — One clear action. Always.

**Content Types:**
- "How [Business Type] Can Maintain 10,000 Customer Relationships with Zero Staff"
- "The Bodiless App: Why Automation-First Beats App-First"
- "I Sent 50,000 Personalized Messages Last Month. Here's What Happened."
- Case studies with specific numbers (open rates, revenue impact, time saved)

### Tone
- Confident, not arrogant
- Specific, not vague
- Human, not corporate
- Bold claims backed by proof

## Stage 2: Interest (They Land on Our Page)

### Landing Page Conversion Principles

**Above the Fold (5 seconds to win)**
- Headline: Clear value prop. What do they GET?
- Subhead: How do they get it? (AI-powered, effortless, etc.)
- Visual: Show the product doing the thing
- CTA: One button. High contrast. Action-oriented verb.
- Social proof: Logos, numbers, or faces

**The Scroll Journey**
1. **Problem acknowledgment** — "You have 1,000 customers but talk to 12"
2. **Solution introduction** — "What if AI handled the relationship logistics?"
3. **How it works** — 3-4 steps, visual, dead simple
4. **Features as benefits** — Not what it does, what they GET
5. **Social proof stack** — Testimonials, case studies, logos, numbers
6. **Objection handling** — FAQ or "But what about..." sections
7. **Risk reversal** — Free trial, no credit card, cancel anytime
8. **Final CTA** — Same as above the fold, repeated

**Conversion Boosters**
- Exit-intent popup (offer value, not desperation)
- Sticky CTA on scroll
- Live chat / AI assistant for questions
- Urgency when authentic (limited beta spots, etc.)

### Landing Page Metrics to Obsess Over
| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Bounce rate | < 40% |
| Time on page | > 2 minutes |
| Scroll depth | > 70% reach bottom |
| CTA click rate | > 5% |
| Free trial conversion | > 15% of visitors |

## Stage 3: Desire (They Want It)

### Social Proof Architecture

**Types of Proof (use all)**
1. **Numbers** — "12,847 automations running" / "2M+ messages sent"
2. **Logos** — Recognizable businesses using Automata
3. **Testimonials** — Real names, real faces, specific results
4. **Case Studies** — Deep dives with before/after
5. **Media Mentions** — "As seen in..."
6. **Community** — Active users, Discord/Slack, user content

**Testimonial Formula**
> "Before Automata, [specific pain]. Now, [specific result with numbers]. [Emotional benefit]."
> — Name, Title, Company (with photo)

### Objection預handling

## Stage 4: Action (They Sign Up)

### Signup Flow Principles

**Reduce friction to zero**
- Email only to start (or Google/Apple SSO)
- No credit card for free trial
- No long forms — collect info progressively
- Instant access — no "check your email" delays

**Signup Page Elements**
- Repeat the core value prop
- Show what they'll see next (screenshot of dashboard)
- Trust badges (security, privacy, no CC required)
- Testimonial or two for reassurance

**Post-Signup Immediate Win**
Within 60 seconds of signing up, they should:
1. See their dashboard
2. Feel oriented (not overwhelmed)
3. Have a clear first action
4. Experience a small win

## Stage 5: Activation (The Aha Moment)

### Defining the Aha Moment

**Automata's Aha Moment:**
> "The moment AI proposes an automation that feels like it read their mind — and they realize they can launch it with one click."

Everything in onboarding leads to this moment. FAST.

### Onboarding Flow

**Step 1: Quick Context (30 seconds)**
- "What type of business?" (dropdown)
- "How many customers?" (range)
- "What's your goal?" (checkboxes)

**Step 2: Data Upload (2 minutes)**
- CSV upload OR
- Manual entry of 5-10 customers OR
- Connect existing tool (future)

**Step 3: AI Analysis (Magic Moment)**
- Show a brief "analyzing" animation
- Build anticipation
- Reveal 5-10 proposed automations with reasoning

**Step 4: First Activation (The Win)**
- Guide them to approve ONE automation
- Show confirmation: "Your first automation is live!"
- Celebrate (confetti, animation, congrats message)

### Onboarding UX Principles
- **Progress indication** — Always show where they are
- **Skip options** — Let power users move fast
- **Tooltips, not walls of text** — Contextual guidance
- **Visual tutorials** — Show, don't tell (no videos longer than 30s)
- **Celebrate wins** — Dopamine hits for completing steps

### Activation Metrics
| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Time to first automation | < 10 minutes |
| % who upload data | > 60% |
| % who see AI proposals | > 50% |
| % who activate first automation | > 30% |

## Stage 6: Retention (They Stay)

### Value Delivery Loop

```
Automation runs → Customer engages → User sees results → User creates more automations
      ↑                                                              ↓
      └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Retention Hooks
- **Dashboard dopamine** — Show metrics improving over time
- **AI suggestions** — Proactively propose new automations
- **Milestone celebrations** — "You've sent 1,000 messages!"
- **Weekly reports** — Email summary of what Automata did for them
- **New features** — Drip new capabilities to maintain novelty

### Churn Prevention
- Identify disengagement early (no login in 7 days)
- Automated re-engagement: "Your automations sent 847 messages while you were away"
- Personal outreach for high-value accounts
- Exit surveys to understand why

## Stage 7: Advocacy (They Spread the Word)

### Referral Mechanics
- **Incentive:** Both referrer and referee get value (extended trial, discount, free automations)
- **Easy sharing:** One-click referral links
- **Track and reward:** Show referral status, celebrate successful referrals

### Organic Advocacy Triggers
- Make results shareable ("Share your stats")
- Encourage case study participation
- Feature users in content (ego boost = sharing)
- Build community (users helping users)

## Copywriting Principles

### Voice
- **Confident:** We know this works.
- **Clear:** No jargon. No fluff.
- **Human:** Talk like a smart friend, not a corporation.
- **Specific:** Numbers > adjectives. Results > promises.

### Headlines Formula
- [Outcome] + [Timeframe] + [Without Pain Point]
- "Scale to 10,000 Customer Relationships Without Hiring a Team"
- "Send Personalized Messages to Every Customer — Automatically"

### CTA Copy
- ❌ "Submit" / "Sign Up" / "Learn More"
- ✅ "Start Free Trial" / "See It In Action" / "Get My First Automation"

Action-oriented. Benefit-focused. First person when possible.

### Power Words
- Free, Instant, Effortless, Automated, Personalized, Scale, Grow, Save, Unlock, Discover

### Banned Words
- Revolutionary, Cutting-edge, Best-in-class, Synergy, Leverage (as verb), "We're excited to announce"

## The 10 Commandments of Automata Marketing

1. **Clarity over cleverness.** If they don't get it in 5 seconds, we failed.
2. **Show, don't tell.** Screenshots > descriptions. Demos > promises.
3. **Specific beats vague.** "12,847 messages sent" > "Thousands of messages"
4. **One CTA per context.** Don't make them choose.
5. **Speed is a feature.** Fast pages, fast onboarding, fast value.
6. **Social proof everywhere.** Humans trust humans.
7. **Handle objections before they arise.** Anticipate doubt.
8. **Celebrate wins.** Dopamine drives behavior.
9. **Reduce friction ruthlessly.** Every field, every click, every second — justify it or kill it.
10. **Test everything.** Opinions are hypotheses. Data is truth.
